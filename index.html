UGA GAMMA PHI BETA TUMBLR!
Gamma Phi Beta was established in 1874 at Syracuse University in New York. The four principles every Gamma Phi Beta lives by is Love, Labor,Learning, and Loyalty. The goal of every Gamma Phi is to inspire the highest type of womanhood through serving each other and the community. Also, Gamma Phis here at UGA are envolved in a multitude of organizations where they hold executive positions. Some organizations Gamma Phi's are involved with are UGA Miracle, HEROs, Relay for Life. They support UGA athletics where some members are Gameday hosts, Deck Dawgs and Hoop Girls.

At the Delta Upsilon chapter of Gamma Phi Beta a philanthropy that Gamma Phi's support throuhout the year is Girls On The Run. Girls On The Run is an organization that builds strong girls through confidence building and mentoring. Members of Gamma Phi Beta serve as coaches,and mentors.

Sisters of Gamma Phi Beta participate locally to The Girls On the Run philanthropy and to the national Philantropy. Gamma phi host thier Chili Cook-Off annually to benefit both aspects of thier philantropy. In the spring Gamma Phi Beta holds it's annual kickball tournament called MoonBall which also benefits these philantropies. In 2016 The Delta Upsilon Chapter was the first chapter to hold MoonBall which was a success. MoonBall is an event in which Gamma phi Beta's envite the Greek community both fraternities and sororities to make a team. Once a team is made a Gamma Phi is appointed coach of that team. The team that wins the MoonBall tournament usually gets concert tickets for each player,and the coach. The Moonball event is not only a great time, but a great way to bring the Greek comunity together for a good cause.
